## Sentence Structure & Grammar (Sarufi)

### Word Order: SVO

**Correct:**

```swahili
Mimi ninakula chakula.    // I am eating food.
Yeye anasoma kitabu.       // S/he is reading a book.
```

### Adjectives Follow Nouns

**Incorrect (reversed order):**

```swahili
zuri kitabu               // ❌ Wrong order
```

**Correct:**

```swahili
kitabu kizuri             // ✅ book good (adjective after noun)
mtoto mzuri               // ✅ child good
```

### Possessives

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | -angu (my) | -etu (our) |
| 2nd | -ako (your) | -enu (your) |
| 3rd | -ake (his/her) | -ao (their) |

Must agree with noun class:
- kitabu **changu** (ki-class → ch-)
- nyumba **yangu** (n-class → y-)
- kitu **changu** (ki-class → ch-)

### Demonstratives

| Distance | Ki- class | Vi- class |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Near | hiki kitabu | hivi vitabu |
| Mid | kile kitabu | vile vitabu |
| Far | hicho kitabu | hivyo vitabu |

### Relative Clauses ("which/that/who" — amba-)

Formed with **amba-** + relative marker (matches noun class), or with the -o- infix on the verb.

| Class | Marker | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 (M-/WA- sg) | -ye | mtu amba**ye** anafanya kazi (the person who is working) |
| 2 (WA- pl) | -o | watu amba**o** wanafanya kazi (the people who are working) |
| 7 (KI-) | -cho | kitabu amba**cho** kipo mezani (the book that is on the table) |
| 9 (N-) | -yo | nyumba amba**yo** ni nzuri (the house that is nice) |

Shorter form (infix -o-): "kitabu kilichopo mezani" (the book that is on the table).

### Question Formation

| Question word | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Nani? | Who? | Nani anakuja? (Who is coming?) |
| Nini? | What? | Unataka nini? (What do you want?) |
| Wapi? | Where? | Unaenda wapi? (Where are you going?) |
| Lini? | When? | Tutaongea lini? (When will we talk?) |
| Kwa nini? | Why? | Kwa nini umechelewa? (Why are you late?) |
| Vipi? / Namna gani? | How? | Tutafanya vipi? (How will we do it?) |
| Ngapi? | How many/much? | Bei ni ngapi? (How much is the price?) |
| Je...? | (yes/no question marker, optional) | Je, umekula? (Have you eaten?) |

Note: Swahili often doesn't invert word order for questions — rising intonation or "je" at the start signals a yes/no question.

### Negation of "to be" and "to have"

| Form | Affirmative | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| to be (am/is/are) | ni | si |
| to have | -na | -na + si → sina, hana, hatuna, etc. |

- Mimi ni mwalimu (I am a teacher) → Mimi si mwalimu (I am not a teacher)
- Nina pesa (I have money) → Sina pesa (I don't have money)
